## üìñ Basic Word Order: SVO

### Subject ‚Üí Verb ‚Üí Object

English has a strict word order. The basic pattern is:

**Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)**

| Subject | Verb | Object |
|---------|------|--------|
| I | eat | breakfast |
| She | reads | books |
| They | play | football |
| The cat | caught | a mouse |
| My mother | loves | cooking |

### Compare with Russian:

In Russian, word order is flexible because of cases:

In English, word order determines meaning:
- Mom loves Dad ‚â† Dad loves Mom (different meanings!)

### üìù Important:
**The subject ALWAYS comes before the verb in statements!**

### Multiple Adjectives - Order Matters!

When using multiple adjectives, follow this order:

**Opinion ‚Üí Size ‚Üí Age ‚Üí Shape ‚Üí Color ‚Üí Origin ‚Üí Material ‚Üí Purpose + NOUN**

| Opinion | Size | Age | Shape | Color | Origin | Material | Purpose | Noun |

**Examples:**

## üìç Place of Adverbs

### Adverbs of Frequency (always, usually, often, sometimes, never)

**Position: BEFORE the main verb, AFTER "be"**

| Subject | Adverb | Verb | Object |
|---------|--------|------|--------|
| I | always | eat | breakfast |
| She | usually | drinks | tea |
| They | sometimes | visit | us |
| We | never | eat | fast food |

**But with "be":**

| Subject | Be | Adverb | |
|---------|-----|--------|---|
| I | am | always | late |
| She | is | usually | happy |
| They | are | never | home |

### Adverbs of Manner (how?)

**Position: AFTER the verb or AFTER the object**

| Subject | Verb | Object | Adverb |
|---------|------|--------|--------|
| She | sings | - | beautifully |
| He | speaks | English | fluently |
| They | did | the work | carefully |
| I | closed | the door | quietly |

### Adverbs of Place and Time

**Basic rule: PLACE before TIME**

| Subject | Verb | Object | Place | Time |
|---------|------|--------|-------|------|
| I | met | her | at the caf√© | yesterday |

## üìç Position of Time Expressions

### Beginning or End of Sentence

Time expressions can go at the beginning OR end:

**At the end (most common):**
- I went to the cinema **yesterday**.
- She will call you **tomorrow**.
- We have a meeting **next week**.

**At the beginning (for emphasis):**
- **Yesterday**, I went to the cinema.
- **Tomorrow**, she will call you.
- **Next week**, we have a meeting.

### Common Time Expressions:

| Time | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Specific time** | at 5 o'clock, at noon, at midnight |
| **Days** | on Monday, on Tuesday, on weekends |
| **Dates** | on January 5th, on my birthday |
| **Parts of day** | in the morning, in the afternoon, at night |
| **Months/Years** | in January, in 2024, in summer |
| **Duration** | for 2 hours, since 2020 |

## üìç Questions - Word Order Changes

### Yes/No Questions

**Auxiliary verb + Subject + Main verb + Object**

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| She speaks English. | Does she speak English? |
| They are students. | Are they students? |
| He can swim. | Can he swim? |
| You have finished. | Have you finished? |

### WH-Questions

**Question word + Auxiliary + Subject + Main verb**

| Question Word | Auxiliary | Subject | Verb | Object/Other |
|---------------|-----------|---------|------|--------------|
| Where | do | you | live | ‚Äî |
| What | does | she | want | ‚Äî |
| When | did | they | arrive | ‚Äî |
| Why | are | you | crying | ‚Äî |
| How | can | I | help | you |

### Exception: Questions about the Subject

When asking about the subject, NO auxiliary needed:

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Who** lives here? | John lives here. |
| **What** happened? | An accident happened. |
| **Who** called you? | My mother called me. |

## üìç Negative Sentences

### Word Order in Negatives

**Subject + Auxiliary + NOT + Main verb**

| Subject | Auxiliary | Not | Verb | Object |
|---------|-----------|-----|------|--------|
| I | do | not | like | coffee |
| She | does | not | speak | French |
| They | did | not | come | yesterday |
| We | have | not | finished | yet |
| He | is | not | working | now |

### Contractions:

- do not = **don't**
- does not = **doesn't**
- did not = **didn't**
- is not = **isn't**
- are not = **aren't**
- have not = **haven't**
- will not = **won't**
- cannot = **can't**

## üìç Complex Sentences

### Two Clauses Connected

**Main clause + Conjunction + Subordinate clause**

| Main Clause | Conjunction | Subordinate Clause |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| I went home | because | I was tired |
| She will call | when | she arrives |
| He passed the exam | although | he didn't study |
| We stayed inside | because | it was raining |

### Subordinate Clause First (with comma):

| Subordinate Clause | , | Main Clause |
|-------------------|---|-------------|
| When she arrives | , | she will call |
| Although he didn't study | , | he passed the exam |
| If it rains | , | we will stay inside |

### Mistake 1: Putting Object Before Verb
‚ùå *I English speak* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **I speak English**
‚ùå *She books reads* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She reads books**

‚ùå *Where you are going?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Where are you going?**
