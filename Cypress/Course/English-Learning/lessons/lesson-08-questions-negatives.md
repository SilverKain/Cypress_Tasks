## ‚ùì Part 1: Yes/No Questions

### Formation Pattern

**Auxiliary Verb + Subject + Main Verb + ?**

### Present Simple Questions

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| You like coffee. | **Do** you like coffee? |
| She speaks English. | **Does** she speak English? |
| They work here. | **Do** they work here? |
| He plays tennis. | **Does** he play tennis? |

**Note:** With he/she/it, use DOES and the verb loses the -s:
- She speak**s** ‚Üí Does she speak?
- He work**s** ‚Üí Does he work?

### Present Continuous Questions

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| You are working. | **Are** you working? |
| She is sleeping. | **Is** she sleeping? |
| They are coming. | **Are** they coming? |

### Past Simple Questions

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| You went home. | **Did** you go home? |
| She finished. | **Did** she finish? |
| They saw the movie. | **Did** they see the movie? |

**Note:** With DID, use the base form of the verb:
- She went ‚Üí Did she **go**?
- He finished ‚Üí Did he **finish**?

### Questions with BE

With "be" as the main verb, no auxiliary needed:

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| You are happy. | **Are** you happy? |
| She was tired. | **Was** she tired? |
| They were at home. | **Were** they at home? |

### Questions with Modal Verbs

Modal verbs become the auxiliary:

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| You can swim. | **Can** you swim? |
| She should go. | **Should** she go? |
| They will come. | **Will** they come? |
| He must leave. | **Must** he leave? |

### Question Words

| Word | Asks about | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|

## üë§ Part 3: Subject Questions

### When asking about the SUBJECT

**NO auxiliary verb needed!**

| Normal Question | Subject Question |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Who**m** did you see? | **Who** saw you? |
| What did she buy? | **What** happened? |
| Who**m** does he love? | **Who** loves him? |

### Compare:

**Object Question (with auxiliary):**
- Who did Mary meet? ‚Üí Mary met **John**.
- What did you buy? ‚Üí I bought **a book**.

**Subject Question (no auxiliary):**
- Who met John? ‚Üí **Mary** met John.
- What happened? ‚Üí **An accident** happened.

### Examples:

| Subject Question | Object Question |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Who called you? | Who did you call? |
| What made you happy? | What did you make? |
| Which book is yours? | Which book did you read? |
| Who wants coffee? | Who do you want to see? |

## ‚ùå Part 4: Negative Sentences

### Formation Pattern

**Subject + Auxiliary + NOT + Main Verb**

### Present Simple Negatives

| Positive | Negative | Contraction |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| I like coffee. | I do not like coffee. | I **don't** like coffee. |
| She speaks French. | She does not speak French. | She **doesn't** speak French. |
| They work here. | They do not work here. | They **don't** work here. |

### Present Continuous Negatives

| Positive | Negative | Contraction |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| I am working. | I am not working. | I'**m not** working. |
| She is sleeping. | She is not sleeping. | She **isn't** sleeping. |
| They are coming. | They are not coming. | They **aren't** coming. |

### Past Simple Negatives

| Positive | Negative | Contraction |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| I went home. | I did not go home. | I **didn't** go home. |
| She finished. | She did not finish. | She **didn't** finish. |
| They saw it. | They did not see it. | They **didn't** see it. |

### Negatives with BE

| Positive | Negative | Contraction |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| I am happy. | I am not happy. | I'**m not** happy. |
| She was tired. | She was not tired. | She **wasn't** tired. |
| They were here. | They were not here. | They **weren't** here. |

### Negatives with Modal Verbs

| Positive | Negative | Contraction |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| I can swim. | I cannot swim. | I **can't** swim. |
| She will come. | She will not come. | She **won't** come. |
| They should go. | They should not go. | They **shouldn't** go. |
| He must leave. | He must not leave. | He **mustn't** leave. |

## üîÑ Part 5: Negative Questions

### Formation

**Auxiliary + n't + Subject + Main Verb + ?**
or
**Auxiliary + Subject + not + Main Verb + ?**

### Examples:

| Full Form | Contracted (common) |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Do you not like it? | **Don't** you like it? |
| Does she not know? | **Doesn't** she know? |
| Did they not come? | **Didn't** they come? |
| Is it not ready? | **Isn't** it ready? |
| Are you not happy? | **Aren't** you happy? |

### Uses of Negative Questions:

**1. Surprise or Disbelief:**
- **Don't** you have a car? (I thought you had one!)
- **Didn't** she tell you? (I'm surprised she didn't!)

**2. Checking information:**
- **Isn't** this the right address?
- **Weren't** you at the party?

**3. Making suggestions (rhetorical):**
- **Wouldn't** it be nice to go out?
- **Why don't** we take a break?

## üè∑Ô∏è Part 6: Question Tags

### What are Question Tags?

Short questions at the end of sentences to confirm information.

**Rule: Positive statement ‚Üí Negative tag / Negative statement ‚Üí Positive tag**

### Examples:

| Statement | Tag | Full Sentence |
|-----------|-----|---------------|
| You are tired, | **aren't you?** | You are tired, aren't you? |
| She likes coffee, | **doesn't she?** | She likes coffee, doesn't she? |
| They went home, | **didn't they?** | They went home, didn't they? |
| It's cold, | **isn't it?** | It's cold, isn't it? |

| Negative Statement | Tag | Full Sentence |
|-------------------|-----|---------------|
| You don't like it, | **do you?** | You don't like it, do you? |
| She isn't coming, | **is she?** | She isn't coming, is she? |
| They can't swim, | **can they?** | They can't swim, can they? |

### Special Cases:

| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| I am right, | **aren't I?** (not "amn't I") |
| Let's go, | **shall we?** |
| Open the door, | **will you?** |
| There is a problem, | **isn't there?** |
| Everyone is here, | **aren't they?** |

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using auxiliary when not needed
‚ùå *What did happen?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **What happened?**
‚ùå *Who does live here?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Who lives here?**

### Mistake 2: Forgetting to change verb form with DO/DOES/DID
‚ùå *Does she speaks English?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Does she speak English?**
‚ùå *Did you went home?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Did you go home?**

### Mistake 3: Using DO with BE
‚ùå *Do you are tired?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Are you tired?**
‚ùå *I don't am happy.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **I'm not happy.**

### Mistake 4: Wrong word order in questions
‚ùå *Where you are going?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **Where are you going?**
‚ùå *What time it is?* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **What time is it?**

### Mistake 5: Double negatives
‚ùå *I don't know nothing.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **I don't know anything.**
‚ùå *She doesn't want no help.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She doesn't want any help.**
