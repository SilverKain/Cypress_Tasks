## ‚è∞ Part 1: Prepositions of Time

### AT / ON / IN

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| **AT** | Specific time, holidays, expressions | at 5 o'clock, at noon, at midnight, at Christmas, at the weekend (UK) |
| **ON** | Days, dates | on Monday, on July 4th, on my birthday, on weekends (US) |
| **IN** | Months, years, seasons, parts of day | in January, in 2024, in summer, in the morning |

### Examples:
- The meeting is **at** 3 PM.
- I was born **on** March 15th.
- We go on vacation **in** summer.
- I wake up **in** the morning.
- See you **on** Friday!
- The movie starts **at** 8:30.

### Exceptions (no preposition):
- **this/last/next/every** + time word
  - I'll call you **next** week. (NOT in next week)
  - I saw her **last** Monday. (NOT on last Monday)
  - We meet **every** morning. (NOT in every morning)

### Other Time Prepositions

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **for** | duration | I studied for 3 hours. |
| **since** | starting point | I've lived here since 2020. |
| **during** | throughout | I slept during the movie. |
| **before** | earlier than | Call me before 6 PM. |
| **after** | later than | Let's meet after lunch. |
| **by** | not later than | Finish it by Friday. |
| **until/till** | up to a time | I'll wait until 5 PM. |
| **from...to** | range | I work from 9 to 5. |

## üìç Part 2: Prepositions of Place

### AT / ON / IN

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| **AT** | Point, specific location | at the bus stop, at the door, at the office, at home |
| **ON** | Surface, street | on the table, on the wall, on Main Street, on the floor |
| **IN** | Enclosed space, area | in the room, in Moscow, in Russia, in the garden |

### Examples:
- She's **at** the bank.
- The book is **on** the shelf.
- He lives **in** London.
- I met her **at** the party.
- The picture is **on** the wall.
- My keys are **in** my bag.

### Special Cases:

| Expression | Preposition |
|------------|-------------|
| at home / at work / at school / at university | AT |
| on the bus / on the train / on a plane | ON |
| in a car / in a taxi | IN |
| at the cinema / at the restaurant / at the station | AT |
| on the first floor | ON |
| in the street (UK) / on the street (US) | IN/ON |
| in the picture / in the photo | IN |
| on the screen / on TV | ON |

### More Place Prepositions

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **above** | higher than (not touching) | The lamp is above the table. |
| **over** | higher than / covering | The bridge is over the river. |
| **below** | lower than (not touching) | The temperature is below zero. |
| **under** | directly below | The cat is under the bed. |
| **between** | in the middle of two | She sat between her parents. |
| **among** | in a group of many | He was among the crowd. |
| **next to / beside** | at the side of | I sat next to Maria. |
| **near** | close to | There's a caf√© near here. |
| **behind** | at the back of | The garden is behind the house. |
| **in front of** | facing | There's a car in front of our house. |
| **opposite** | facing on the other side | The bank is opposite the post office. |

## üö∂ Part 3: Prepositions of Movement

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **to** | direction, destination | I'm going **to** the store. |
| **from** | origin, starting point | She's from **from** Italy. |
| **into** | entering | He walked **into** the room. |
| **out of** | leaving | She ran **out of** the house. |
| **through** | from one side to another | We drove **through** the tunnel. |
| **across** | from one side to the other (surface) | Walk **across** the street. |
| **along** | following a path | Walk **along** the river. |
| **around** | in a circular motion | She walked **around** the park. |
| **up** | to a higher place | Climb **up** the stairs. |
| **down** | to a lower place | Run **down** the hill. |
| **past** | by, beyond | Walk **past** the church. |
| **towards** | in the direction of | He ran **towards** me. |

### TO vs AT vs IN (Movement vs Position)

| Movement (TO) | Position (AT/IN) |
|---------------|------------------|
| I'm going **to** work. | I'm **at** work. |
| She went **to** bed. | She's **in** bed. |
| We flew **to** Paris. | We are **in** Paris. |

## üîó Part 5: Connectors and Linking Words

### Adding Information (–∏, —Ç–∞–∫–∂–µ, –∫—Ä–æ–º–µ —Ç–æ–≥–æ)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **and** | I like coffee and tea. |
| **also** | She also speaks French. |
| **too** (end of sentence) | I like it too. |
| **as well** (end of sentence) | He came as well. |
| **in addition** | In addition, we need more time. |
| **moreover** (formal) | Moreover, the costs are too high. |
| **furthermore** (formal) | Furthermore, there are other issues. |
| **besides** | Besides, it's too expensive. |

### Contrasting (–Ω–æ, –æ–¥–Ω–∞–∫–æ, –Ω–µ—Å–º–æ—Ç—Ä—è –Ω–∞)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **but** | I like him, but he doesn't like me. |
| **however** | I wanted to go. However, I was too tired. |
| **although/though** | Although it was raining, we went out. |
| **even though** | Even though he's rich, he's not happy. |
| **despite / in spite of** + noun/-ing | Despite the rain, we went out. |
| **nevertheless** (formal) | The task was hard. Nevertheless, we finished. |
| **on the other hand** | On the other hand, it could be useful. |
| **while / whereas** | She's tall, while her sister is short. |

### Cause and Reason (–ø–æ—Ç–æ–º—É —á—Ç–æ, —Ç–∞–∫ –∫–∞–∫)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **because** | I stayed home because I was sick. |
| **because of** + noun | Because of the rain, we stayed in. |
| **since / as** | Since you're here, let's start. |
| **due to** + noun | The delay was due to bad weather. |
| **therefore** | It was late; therefore, I went home. |
| **so** | I was tired, so I went to bed. |
| **as a result** | As a result, we missed the flight. |
| **consequently** (formal) | Consequently, the project failed. |

### Purpose (—á—Ç–æ–±—ã, –¥–ª—è —Ç–æ–≥–æ —á—Ç–æ–±—ã)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **to** + verb | I came to help. |
| **in order to** + verb | She studies in order to pass the exam. |
| **so that** + clause | I left early so that I wouldn't be late. |
| **so as to** + verb | He whispered so as not to wake the baby. |

### Giving Examples (–Ω–∞–ø—Ä–∏–º–µ—Ä)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **for example** | Some fruits, for example apples, are healthy. |
| **for instance** | For instance, you could try yoga. |
| **such as** | Countries such as Japan and Korea. |
| **like** (informal) | I like sports like football and tennis. |
| **including** | Everyone came, including the boss. |

### Sequence (–≤–æ-–ø–µ—Ä–≤—ã—Ö, –∑–∞—Ç–µ–º, –Ω–∞–∫–æ–Ω–µ—Ü)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **first / firstly** | First, turn on the computer. |
| **second / secondly** | Second, open the program. |
| **then / next** | Then, click on the icon. |
| **after that** | After that, enter your password. |
| **finally / lastly** | Finally, press Enter. |
| **in the end** | In the end, everything worked out. |

### Summarizing (–≤ –∑–∞–∫–ª—é—á–µ–Ω–∏–µ, –≤ —Ü–µ–ª–æ–º)

| Connector | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **in conclusion** | In conclusion, I support this idea. |
| **to sum up** | To sum up, there are three main points. |
| **in summary** | In summary, the project was successful. |
| **overall** | Overall, it was a good experience. |
| **all in all** | All in all, I enjoyed the trip. |

## üìù Part 6: Punctuation with Connectors

### Comma Rules

| Position | Punctuation | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Beginning of sentence | Connector + comma | **However,** he didn't listen. |
| Middle of sentence | semicolon + connector + comma | I was tired**; however,** I kept working. |
| With "but, and, so" | comma before | I tried**,** but I failed. |

### Examples:
- **Although** it was cold, we went outside.
- I was hungry**. Therefore**, I ordered pizza.
- She's smart**;** moreover, she's hard-working.
- We left early **because** it was getting dark.
