## üó£ Difficult Consonants / –°–ª–æ–∂–Ω—ã–µ —Å–æ–≥–ª–∞—Å–Ω—ã–µ

### **The TH Sounds (Œ∏ and √∞)**

English has TWO "th" sounds. Neither exists in Russian!

#### **Voiceless TH /Œ∏/** (–≥–ª—É—Ö–æ–π)
**How to make it:** 
1. Put your tongue between your teeth
2. Blow air out
3. Do NOT vibrate your vocal cords

**Words:** think, three, thank, bath, tooth, Thursday

**Practice sentences:**
- I **th**ink **th**is is **th**e right answer.
- **Th**ree **th**ousand **th**ings.
- **Th**ank you very much.

#### **Voiced TH /√∞/** (–∑–≤–æ–Ω–∫–∏–π)
**How to make it:**
1. Same tongue position as /Œ∏/
2. But vibrate your vocal cords (like humming)

**Words:** the, this, that, these, those, mother, weather

**Practice sentences:**
- **Th**is is **th**e book **th**at I want.
- **Th**e wea**th**er is nice **th**ese days.
- My mo**th**er and fa**th**er are **th**ere.

#### **Common Mistakes:**
| Wrong | Correct | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| "ze book" | "the book" | /√∞/ not /z/ |
| "sink" | "think" | /Œ∏/ not /s/ |
| "tree" | "three" | /Œ∏/ not /t/ |
| "wiz" | "with" | /√∞/ not /z/ |

### **The W Sound /w/**

This sound doesn't exist in Russian. It's NOT the same as V!

**How to make it:**
1. Round your lips into a small circle (like kissing)
2. Keep your teeth APART
3. Make a sound while opening lips

**Words:** water, we, what, where, when, why, want, with, work, world

**Practice: W vs V**
| W sound | V sound |
|---------|---------|
| wine /wa…™n/ | vine /va…™n/ |
| west /west/ | vest /vest/ |
| wet /wet/ | vet /vet/ |
| we /wiÀê/ | V (letter) /viÀê/ |

**Practice sentences:**
- **W**hat do you **w**ant?
- **W**here **w**ere you **w**orking?
- The **w**eather **w**as **w**onderful on **W**ednesday.

### **The R Sound /r/**

English R is completely different from Russian "—Ä"!

**How to make it:**
1. Curl your tongue back (but don't touch anything!)
2. Keep the tip of your tongue in the air
3. DO NOT trill or roll the R

**Words:** red, run, right, very, more, car, after

**Practice sentences:**
- The **r**ed ca**r** is **r**eally fast.
- **R**emember to **r**ead the **r**eport.
- My b**r**other a**rr**ived in Ap**r**il.

**Common mistakes:**
- Don't tap or roll the R (no "—Ä—Ä—Ä")
- Don't make it too strong at the end of words

---

### **The H Sound /h/**

This is a soft breath, NOT like Russian "—Ö"!

**How to make it:**
1. Open your mouth
2. Breathe out (like fogging a mirror)
3. Add voice immediately after

**Words:** hello, house, happy, have, he, her, his, how, here

**Practice sentences:**
- **H**ello! **H**ow are you?
- **H**e has a **h**ouse on the **h**ill.
- I **h**ope you're **h**appy **h**ere.

**Warning:** Don't add H where it doesn't belong!
- "apple" NOT "happle"
- "I am" NOT "I ham"

## üìä Word Stress / –£–¥–∞—Ä–µ–Ω–∏–µ –≤ —Å–ª–æ–≤–∞—Ö

English words have STRESS (—É–¥–∞—Ä–µ–Ω–∏–µ). One syllable is louder and longer.

### **Basic Rules:**

**Rule 1: Most 2-syllable NOUNS - stress on FIRST syllable**
- **TA**ble, **AP**ple, **MU**sic, **WA**ter, **DOC**tor

**Rule 2: Most 2-syllable VERBS - stress on SECOND syllable**
- be**GIN**, a**RRIVE**, de**CIDE**, en**JOY**, ex**PLAIN**

**Rule 3: Words ending in -tion, -sion - stress before these endings**
- in-for-**MA**-tion, de-ci-**SION**, edu-**CA**-tion

**Rule 4: Words ending in -ic - stress on the syllable before -ic**
- fan-**TAS**-tic, dra-**MAT**-ic, e-lec-**TRON**-ic
