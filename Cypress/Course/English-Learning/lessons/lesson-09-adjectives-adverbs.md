## üìñ Part 1: What's the Difference?

### Adjectives describe NOUNS (things, people)
### Adverbs describe VERBS, ADJECTIVES, or other ADVERBS

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| She is a **beautiful** singer. | She sings **beautifully**. |
| He is a **slow** driver. | He drives **slowly**. |
| It was a **quick** decision. | He decided **quickly**. |
| They are **careful** workers. | They work **carefully**. |

### Key Questions:
- **Adjective:** WHAT kind? (What kind of singer? ‚Üí beautiful)
- **Adverb:** HOW? (How does she sing? ‚Üí beautifully)

## üî§ Part 2: Forming Adverbs

### Rule 1: Add -LY to most adjectives

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| slow | slow**ly** |
| quick | quick**ly** |
| careful | careful**ly** |
| beautiful | beautiful**ly** |
| serious | serious**ly** |
| perfect | perfect**ly** |
| quiet | quiet**ly** |
| polite | polite**ly** |

### Rule 2: Adjectives ending in -Y ‚Üí change Y to -ILY

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| happy | happ**ily** |
| easy | eas**ily** |
| angry | angr**ily** |
| lucky | luck**ily** |
| heavy | heav**ily** |
| lazy | laz**ily** |

### Rule 3: Adjectives ending in -LE ‚Üí change -LE to -LY

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| simple | simp**ly** |
| terrible | terrib**ly** |
| comfortable | comfortab**ly** |
| possible | possib**ly** |
| probable | probab**ly** |

### Rule 4: Adjectives ending in -IC ‚Üí add -ALLY

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| basic | basic**ally** |
| automatic | automatic**ally** |
| dramatic | dramatic**ally** |
| tragic | tragic**ally** |
| **Exception:** public | public**ly** |

### Rule 5: Irregular Adverbs (same form or different)

| Adjective | Adverb |
|-----------|--------|
| good | **well** |
| fast | **fast** |
| hard | **hard** |
| late | **late** |
| early | **early** |
| daily | **daily** |
| high | **high** |
| low | **low** |

**‚ö†Ô∏è Careful:**
- hard (adverb) = with effort: *He works hard.*
- hardly (adverb) = almost not: *I hardly know him.*
- late (adverb) = not on time: *He arrived late.*
- lately (adverb) = recently: *Have you seen him lately?*

## üìç Part 3: Position of Adjectives

### Before nouns:
- a **beautiful** day
- an **interesting** book
- a **tall** building

### After linking verbs (be, seem, look, feel, become, appear):
- The sky is **blue**.
- She looks **happy**.
- The food smells **delicious**.
- He seems **tired**.
- She became **famous**.

### Order of Multiple Adjectives:

**Opinion ‚Üí Size ‚Üí Age ‚Üí Shape ‚Üí Color ‚Üí Origin ‚Üí Material ‚Üí Purpose**

| Opinion | Size | Age | Shape | Color | Origin | Material | Purpose | Noun |
|---------|------|-----|-------|-------|--------|----------|---------|------|
| lovely | big | old | round | blue | French | wooden | dining | table |
| nice | small | new | - | red | Italian | leather | - | bag |

Examples:
- a beautiful **old Italian** painting
- an expensive **new Japanese** car
- a lovely **little round** table

## üìç Part 4: Position of Adverbs

### Adverbs of Manner (how?) - Usually AFTER the verb/object

- She sings **beautifully**.
- He speaks English **fluently**.
- They completed the work **successfully**.

### Adverbs of Frequency - BEFORE main verb, AFTER "be"

| Subject | Adverb | Verb |
|---------|--------|------|
| I | always | eat breakfast |
| She | usually | drinks tea |
| They | sometimes | visit us |
| We | never | eat fast food |

| Subject | Be | Adverb |
|---------|-----|--------|
| I | am | always late |
| She | is | usually happy |
| They | are | never home |

**Frequency adverbs (from most to least):**
- always (100%)
- usually (90%)
- often (70%)
- sometimes (50%)
- rarely/seldom (10%)
- never (0%)

### Adverbs of Degree - BEFORE adjective/adverb

- She is **very** beautiful.
- He runs **extremely** fast.
- It's **quite** cold today.
- I'm **really** tired.
- The movie was **incredibly** boring.

**Common degree adverbs:**
very, really, extremely, quite, fairly, pretty, rather, a bit, slightly, almost, nearly, completely, absolutely, totally

### Adverbs of Time - Usually END of sentence

- I will call you **tomorrow**.
- She arrived **yesterday**.
- We met **last week**.
- They're coming **soon**.

## üìä Part 5: Comparatives and Superlatives

### Adjectives

#### Short adjectives (1 syllable): add -ER / -EST

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| tall | tall**er** | the tall**est** |
| old | old**er** | the old**est** |
| fast | fast**er** | the fast**est** |
| big | bigg**er** | the bigg**est** |
| hot | hott**er** | the hott**est** |

#### Adjectives ending in -Y: change Y to -IER / -IEST

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| happy | happ**ier** | the happ**iest** |
| easy | eas**ier** | the eas**iest** |
| funny | funn**ier** | the funn**iest** |
| early | earl**ier** | the earl**iest** |

#### Long adjectives (2+ syllables): use MORE / THE MOST

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| beautiful | **more** beautiful | **the most** beautiful |
| expensive | **more** expensive | **the most** expensive |
| interesting | **more** interesting | **the most** interesting |
| comfortable | **more** comfortable | **the most** comfortable |

#### Irregular adjectives

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| good | **better** | **the best** |
| bad | **worse** | **the worst** |
| far | **farther/further** | **the farthest/furthest** |
| little | **less** | **the least** |
| much/many | **more** | **the most** |

### Adverbs

#### Short adverbs: add -ER / -EST

| Adverb | Comparative | Superlative |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| fast | fast**er** | fast**est** |
| hard | hard**er** | hard**est** |
| early | earl**ier** | earl**iest** |

#### Adverbs ending in -LY: use MORE / MOST

| Adverb | Comparative | Superlative |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| quickly | **more** quickly | **most** quickly |
| carefully | **more** carefully | **most** carefully |
| beautifully | **more** beautifully | **most** beautifully |

#### Irregular adverbs

|--------|-------------|-------------|

| badly | **worse** | **worst** |
| far | **farther/further** | **farthest/furthest** |

## üîó Part 6: Comparison Structures

### Comparing two things

**as ... as** (equal comparison)
- She is **as tall as** her brother.
- He runs **as fast as** I do.
- This book is **as interesting as** that one.

**not as ... as / not so ... as** (unequal)
- She is **not as tall as** her brother.
- He doesn't run **as fast as** I do.

**... than** (different)
- She is **taller than** her brother.
- He runs **faster than** I do.
- This is **more expensive than** that.

### Comparing more than two things

**the ... of/in** (superlative)
- She is **the tallest** in the class.
- This is **the most beautiful** of all.
- He runs **the fastest** of all the athletes.

### Progressive comparison

**... and ...** (increasing/decreasing)
- The weather is getting **colder and colder**.
- She's becoming **more and more confident**.
- He works **harder and harder**.

**the ... the ...** (parallel change)
- **The more** you practice, **the better** you get.
- **The older** I get, **the wiser** I become.
- **The sooner**, **the better**.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using adjective instead of adverb
‚ùå *She sings beautiful.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She sings beautifully.**
‚ùå *He drives careful.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **He drives carefully.**

### Mistake 2: Using adverb instead of adjective after BE
‚ùå *She is beautifully.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She is beautiful.**
‚ùå *The food smells deliciously.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **The food smells delicious.**

### Mistake 3: Good vs Well
‚ùå *She speaks English good.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She speaks English well.**
‚ùå *I don't feel good.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **I don't feel well.** (health)
*(But: "I feel good" is OK for emotions)*

### Mistake 4: Double comparison
‚ùå *more better* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **better**
‚ùå *most tallest* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **the tallest**
‚ùå *more easier* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **easier**

### Mistake 5: Missing "the" in superlatives
‚ùå *She is tallest in the class.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She is the tallest in the class.**

### Mistake 6: Confusing hard/hardly, late/lately
‚ùå *He works hardly.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **He works hard.**
‚ùå *She arrived lately.* ‚Üí ‚úÖ **She arrived late.**
