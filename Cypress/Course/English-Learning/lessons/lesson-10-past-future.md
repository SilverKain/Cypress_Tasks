### When to use:
- **Completed actions in the past**
- **Specific time in the past**
- **Sequences of past events**

## ‚è™ Part 2: Past Continuous

### When to use:
- **Action in progress at a specific time in the past**
- **Background action when another action interrupted it**
- **Two actions happening at the same time in the past**

### Structure

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **+** | S + was/were + V-ing | I **was working** at 5 PM. |
| **-** | S + wasn't/weren't + V-ing | I **wasn't working** at 5 PM. |
| **?** | Was/Were + S + V-ing? | **Were** you **working** at 5 PM? |

### Past Simple vs Past Continuous

| Past Simple | Past Continuous |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Completed action | Action in progress |
| I ate dinner. | I was eating dinner when he called. |
| She arrived at 8. | She was arriving when it started to rain. |

### Combined use:

**When/While + Past Continuous, Past Simple**
or
**Past Simple + when + Past Continuous**

- **While** I **was watching** TV, the phone **rang**.
- The phone **rang** **while** I **was watching** TV.
- **When** he **arrived**, we **were having** dinner.
- We **were having** dinner **when** he **arrived**.

### Examples:
- At 9 PM yesterday, I **was studying**.
- They **were playing** football when it started to rain.
- **What were you doing** at midnight?

## ‚è≥ Part 3: Present Perfect

### When to use:
- **Past action with present result**
- **Experience (ever/never)**
- **Recent past (just, already, yet)**
- **Unfinished time period (today, this week)**
- **Duration (for, since)**

### Structure

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **+** | S + have/has + V3 | I **have finished** my work. |
| **-** | S + haven't/hasn't + V3 | I **haven't finished** my work. |
| **?** | Have/Has + S + V3? | **Have** you **finished** your work? |

### Past Participle (V3)

| Infinitive | Past Simple | Past Participle |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| be | was/were | been |
| go | went | gone |
| do | did | done |
| see | saw | seen |
| eat | ate | eaten |
| write | wrote | written |
| take | took | taken |
| give | gave | given |
| work | worked | worked |
| live | lived | lived |

### Time Expressions:

| With Present Perfect | Meaning |
|---------------------|---------|
| just | —Ç–æ–ª—å–∫–æ —á—Ç–æ |
| already | —É–∂–µ |
| yet (questions, negatives) | –µ—â—ë, —É–∂–µ |
| ever | –∫–æ–≥–¥–∞-–ª–∏–±–æ |
| never | –Ω–∏–∫–æ–≥–¥–∞ |
| recently/lately | –Ω–µ–¥–∞–≤–Ω–æ |
| since + point in time | —Å (2020, Monday) |
| for + period of time | –≤ —Ç–µ—á–µ–Ω–∏–µ (3 years) |

### Examples:
- I **have just eaten** lunch.
- She **has already left**.
- **Have** you finished **yet**?
- I **have never been** to Japan.
- **Have** you **ever tried** sushi?
- We **have lived** here **for** 10 years.
- I **have known** her **since** 2015.

### Present Perfect vs Past Simple

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|-----------------|-------------|
| No specific time / Result important | Specific time |
| I **have lost** my keys. (I still can't find them) | I **lost** my keys yesterday. (specific time) |
| She **has visited** Paris. (experience - when not important) | She **visited** Paris in 2019. (specific time) |
| I **haven't eaten** today. (today not finished) | I **didn't eat** yesterday. (yesterday finished) |

## ‚è© Part 4: Future Simple (will)

### When to use:
- **Predictions** (I think, probably, maybe)
- **Spontaneous decisions**
- **Promises**
- **Offers**
- **Facts about the future**

### Structure

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **+** | S + will + V (base) | I **will help** you. |
| **-** | S + won't + V | I **won't forget**. |
| **?** | Will + S + V? | **Will** you **come**? |

### Contractions:
- I will = I'll
- will not = won't

### Examples:
- I think it **will rain** tomorrow. (prediction)
- Wait, I**'ll open** the door for you. (spontaneous decision)
- I **will always love** you. (promise)
- Don't worry. I**'ll help** you. (offer)
- The meeting **will start** at 10 AM. (scheduled fact)

## ‚è© Part 5: Going to

### When to use:
- **Plans and intentions** (already decided)
- **Predictions with evidence** (you can see it will happen)

### Structure

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **+** | S + am/is/are + going to + V | I **am going to study** tonight. |
| **-** | S + am/is/are + not going to + V | I **am not going to watch** TV. |
| **?** | Am/Is/Are + S + going to + V? | **Are** you **going to come**? |

### Examples:
- I **am going to visit** my parents this weekend. (plan)
- We **are going to move** to a new apartment. (intention)
- Look at those clouds! It **is going to rain**. (evidence)
- Be careful! You **are going to fall**! (evidence)

### Will vs Going to

| Will | Going to |
|------|----------|
| Decision made NOW | Decision made BEFORE |
| A: "The phone is ringing!" B: "I'll answer it." | I'm going to call her tomorrow. (planned) |
| Prediction (opinion) | Prediction (evidence) |
| I think it will rain. | Look at those clouds. It's going to rain. |

---

## ‚è© Part 6: Present Continuous for Future

### When to use:
- **Fixed arrangements** (diary, appointments)
- Often involves other people

### Examples:
- I **am meeting** John at 3 PM. (appointment)
- We **are flying** to London tomorrow. (booked)
- **Are** you **coming** to the party tonight? (arranged)

## üìä Summary Table

| Tense | Use | Example |
|-------|-----|---------|
| **Past Simple** | Completed past action, specific time | I worked yesterday. |
| **Past Continuous** | Action in progress in past | I was working at 5 PM. |
| **Present Perfect** | Past action connected to now | I have finished. |
| **Future (will)** | Predictions, decisions, promises | I will help you. |
| **Going to** | Plans, intentions, evidence | I am going to study. |
| **Present Continuous (future)** | Fixed arrangements | I am meeting him tomorrow. |
