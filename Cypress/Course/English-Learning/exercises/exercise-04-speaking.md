## üó£Ô∏è Part 1: Self-Introduction / –°–∞–º–æ–ø—Ä–µ–∑–µ–Ω—Ç–∞—Ü–∏—è

### **Exercise 1.1: Basic Introduction**

Practice saying this about yourself (fill in the blanks):

> Hello! My name is ___. I am ___ years old. I am from ___. I live in ___. I work as a ___ / I am a student at ___. I like ___ and ___. Nice to meet you!

**Practice Tips:**
- Record yourself and listen back
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Smile while speaking!

### **Exercise 1.2: Extended Introduction**

Now add more details:

> In my free time, I enjoy ___. My favorite hobby is ___. I have ___ (siblings/no siblings). I have been learning English for ___. I want to learn English because ___. One interesting fact about me is ___.

### **Exercise 1.3: Model Dialogues**

Practice these conversations:

**Dialogue A: Meeting Someone New**

A: Hi! I'm Alex. What's your name?
B: Hi Alex! I'm ___. Nice to meet you.
A: Nice to meet you too! Where are you from?
B: I'm from ___. And you?
A: I'm from London. What do you do?
B: I work as a ___. / I'm a student.
A: That sounds interesting!

**Dialogue B: At a Party**

A: Great party, isn't it?
B: Yes, it is! Are you a friend of the host?
A: Yes, we work together. How about you?
B: I'm ___'s classmate. We met at university.
A: Oh, nice! What did you study?
B: I studied ___. What about you?
A: I'm in marketing. Can I get you a drink?
B: Sure, thanks! I'll have ___.

## üí¨ Part 2: Everyday Conversations / –ü–æ–≤—Å–µ–¥–Ω–µ–≤–Ω—ã–µ —Ä–∞–∑–≥–æ–≤–æ—Ä—ã

### **Exercise 2.1: At a Caf√©**

Practice ordering:

**Waiter:** Good morning! What can I get for you?
**You:** I'd like ___, please.
**Waiter:** Anything else?
**You:** Yes, can I have ___ as well?
**Waiter:** Of course. For here or to go?
**You:** For here, please.
**Waiter:** That'll be $___. 
**You:** Here you go.
**Waiter:** Thank you. Have a nice day!
**You:** Thank you, you too!

**Useful phrases:**
- I'd like... / Can I have...?
- Could I get a...?
- I'll have the...
- What do you recommend?
- Does this have nuts/gluten/dairy?
- Can I pay by card?

### **Exercise 2.2: Shopping**

**Shop Assistant:** Hi! Can I help you?
**You:** Yes, I'm looking for ___.
**Assistant:** Sure. What size are you?
**You:** I'm a size ___.
**Assistant:** Here are some options. Would you like to try them on?
**You:** Yes, please. Where is the fitting room?
**Assistant:** It's just over there.
*(After trying on)*
**You:** I'll take this one. How much is it?
**Assistant:** It's $___. Would you like a bag?
**You:** Yes, please.

**Useful phrases:**
- I'm just looking, thanks.
- Do you have this in a different color/size?
- Can I try this on?
- It's too big/small.
- I'll take it.
- Do you accept credit cards?
- Can I get a receipt?

### **Exercise 2.3: Asking for Directions**

**You:** Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to ___?
**Person:** Sure! Go straight for two blocks, then turn left.
**You:** Turn left at the corner?
**Person:** Yes, exactly. Then walk for about 5 minutes. It's on your right.
**You:** Is it far from here?
**Person:** No, it's about a 10-minute walk.
**You:** Thank you so much!
**Person:** You're welcome. Have a good day!

**Practice describing these locations:**
- the train station
- the nearest supermarket
- a pharmacy
- a good restaurant
- the city center

**Useful phrases:**
- Excuse me, where is...?
- How do I get to...?
- Is it far from here?
- Can you show me on the map?
- Go straight / Turn left / Turn right
- It's on the corner / next to / opposite

## üìû Part 3: Phone Conversations / –¢–µ–ª–µ—Ñ–æ–Ω–Ω—ã–µ —Ä–∞–∑–≥–æ–≤–æ—Ä—ã

### **Exercise 3.1: Making an Appointment**

**Receptionist:** Good morning, Dr. Smith's office. How can I help you?
**You:** Hello, I'd like to make an appointment, please.
**Receptionist:** Of course. Is this for a routine checkup or a specific problem?
**You:** ___.
**Receptionist:** When would you like to come in?
**You:** Is ___ available?
**Receptionist:** Let me check... Yes, we have 10 AM and 2 PM available.
**You:** I'll take the ___ appointment, please.
**Receptionist:** May I have your name?
**You:** It's ___.
**Receptionist:** Perfect. We'll see you on ___ at ___.
**You:** Thank you. Goodbye!

### **Exercise 3.2: Calling a Friend**

**You:** Hey! It's ___. How are you?
**Friend:** Oh, hi! I'm good, thanks. What's up?
**You:** I was wondering if you're free this weekend.
**Friend:** Let me think... I'm free on Saturday. Why?
**You:** Do you want to ___?
**Friend:** That sounds great! What time?
**You:** How about ___ o'clock?
**Friend:** Perfect! See you then.
**You:** Great! See you Saturday!

### **Exercise 3.3: Leaving a Voicemail**

Practice leaving this message:

> Hi, this is ___. I'm calling about ___. Could you please call me back at ___? I'm available ___. Thank you!

## üé§ Part 4: Discussion Topics / –¢–µ–º—ã –¥–ª—è –æ–±—Å—É–∂–¥–µ–Ω–∏—è

### **Exercise 4.1: Giving Opinions**

Learn these phrases and practice using them:

**Agreeing:**
- I agree.
- That's true.
- Exactly!
- I think so too.
- You're absolutely right.

**Disagreeing (politely):**
- I'm not sure about that.
- I see your point, but...
- I'm afraid I disagree.
- That's one way to look at it, but...
- I understand, however...

**Expressing your opinion:**
- I think that...
- In my opinion...
- From my point of view...
- I believe that...
- As I see it...

### **Exercise 4.2: Topic Cards**

Practice speaking about these topics for 1-2 minutes each:

**Card 1: Your Hometown**
- Where is it located?
- What is it famous for?
- What do you like about it?
- What would you change?

**Card 2: Your Job/Studies**
- What do you do?
- Why did you choose this?
- What do you like about it?
- What are your future plans?

**Card 3: Technology**
- How has technology changed your life?
- What gadgets do you use daily?
- What are the pros and cons of social media?

**Card 4: Travel**
- Where have you traveled?
- Where would you like to go?
- Do you prefer cities or nature?
- What's the best trip you've ever had?

**Card 5: Learning English**
- Why are you learning English?
- What's the hardest part?
- What's your favorite way to practice?
- What are your language goals?

### **Exercise 4.3: Debate Practice**

Choose a topic and practice arguing both sides:

**Topics:**
1. Working from home is better than working in an office.
2. Everyone should learn a second language.
3. Social media does more harm than good.
4. Reading books is better than watching movies.
5. City life is better than country life.

**Structure:**
- State your opinion: "I believe that..."
- Give reason 1: "Firstly,..."
- Give reason 2: "Secondly,..."
- Give reason 3: "Finally,..."
- Conclusion: "In conclusion,..."

## üé¨ Part 5: Role-Play Scenarios / –†–æ–ª–µ–≤—ã–µ —Å—Ü–µ–Ω–∞—Ä–∏–∏

### **Scenario 1: Job Interview**

Practice being both the interviewer and the candidate:

**Interviewer:**
- Tell me about yourself.
- Why do you want this job?
- What are your strengths and weaknesses?
- Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
- Do you have any questions for me?

**Candidate (your answers):**
- Well, I'm ___ and I have experience in...
- I'm interested in this position because...
- My main strength is... One area I'm working on is...
- In five years, I hope to...
- Yes, I'd like to know about...

### **Scenario 2: Hotel Check-In**

**Receptionist:** Good evening. Welcome to the Grand Hotel. How may I help you?
**Guest:** Hello, I have a reservation under the name ___.
**Receptionist:** Let me check... Yes, a double room for three nights?
**Guest:** That's right.
**Receptionist:** May I see your ID and credit card, please?
**Guest:** Here you are.
**Receptionist:** Thank you. Your room is on the 5th floor. Here's your key.
**Guest:** Thank you. What time is breakfast?
**Receptionist:** Breakfast is from 7 to 10 AM in the restaurant on the ground floor.
**Guest:** Great. And is there Wi-Fi in the room?
**Receptionist:** Yes, it's free. The password is on the desk.
**Guest:** Perfect, thank you!

### **Scenario 3: At the Doctor's**

**Doctor:** Hello! What seems to be the problem?
**Patient:** I've been feeling ___ for the past few days.
**Doctor:** I see. Do you have any other symptoms?
**Patient:** Yes, I also have ___.
**Doctor:** When did this start?
**Patient:** About ___ days ago.
**Doctor:** Have you taken any medication?
**Patient:** I tried taking ___, but it didn't help.
**Doctor:** Let me examine you... I think you have ___. I'm going to prescribe some medicine.
**Patient:** How often should I take it?
**Doctor:** Take it ___ times a day after meals.
**Patient:** Thank you, Doctor.

### **Exercise 6.1: Tongue Twisters**

Practice saying these quickly and clearly:

**Level 1 (Easy):**
1. She sells seashells by the seashore.
2. Red lorry, yellow lorry.
3. Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers.

**Level 2 (Medium):**
4. How much wood would a woodchuck chuck if a woodchuck could chuck wood?
5. I scream, you scream, we all scream for ice cream!
6. Betty Botter bought some butter, but she said the butter's bitter.

**Level 3 (Hard):**
7. The sixth sick sheikh's sixth sheep's sick.
8. Whether the weather is cold, or whether the weather is hot, we'll weather the weather, whatever the weather.

### **Exercise 6.2: Minimal Pairs**

Practice distinguishing and pronouncing these similar sounds:

**L vs R:**
- light / right
- long / wrong
- fly / fry
- cloud / crowd

**V vs W:**
- vine / wine
- vet / wet
- vest / west
- very / wary

**TH sounds:**
- think / sink
- three / free
- breath / breeze
- bath / bass

### **Exercise 6.3: Stress Practice**

Say these words with correct stress (stressed syllable in CAPITALS):

- PHOtograph / phoTOgraphy / photoGRAphic
- ECOnomy / ecoNOMic
- TELephone / teLEphone
- PREsent (noun) / preSENT (verb)
- REcord (noun) / reCORD (verb)

### **Challenge 1: One-Minute Speech**
Choose any topic and speak for exactly one minute without stopping.

### **Challenge 2: Story Chain**
Start a story with "Yesterday, I was walking in the park when suddenly..."
Continue for as long as you can.

### **Challenge 3: Question Marathon**
Ask yourself 20 different questions and answer them all in English.

### **Challenge 4: Describe Everything**
Look around the room and describe 10 objects in detail.

### **Challenge 5: Future Plans**
Talk about your plans for the next year in as much detail as possible.
